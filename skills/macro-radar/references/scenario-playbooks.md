# Scenario Playbooks

Pre-defined action plans for major risk scenarios. When a scenario triggers, execute the playbook without hesitation — the time to decide is now, not during the crisis.

## Scenario 1: Japan Debt Crisis ("The Black Swan")

**Trigger**: Japan 10Y yield > 1.5% AND rapid acceleration (>50bps in 30 days)

**Mechanism**: Japanese institutions sell US Treasuries → US yields spike → equity multiple compression → forced selling cascade → liquidity crisis

**Playbook**:
1. Immediately increase cash to 30% (sell weakest positions first)
2. Add gold (GLD/IAU) to 10-15% if underweight
3. Reduce long-duration growth stocks (high P/E names most vulnerable)
4. KEEP: defense stocks, commodity producers, gold miners
5. AVOID: buying the first dip — wait for VIX > 40 for quality entries
6. DEPLOY CASH when: VIX peaks + Japan yield stabilizes + US Treasury auction shows demand recovery

**Historical analog**: 2022 UK Gilt crisis — rapid yield spike → forced pension selling → cascading liquidation

## Scenario 2: AI Bubble Pop

**Trigger**: Major AI company misses earnings + AI capex cuts announced + semiconductor orders decline

**Mechanism**: AI narrative breaks → NVDA/AMD/AVGO sell off 30-50% → contagion to cloud (AMZN/MSFT/GOOGL) → broader tech selloff → retail panic

**Playbook**:
1. If portfolio is >40% AI-exposed: trim to 25% immediately
2. KEEP: NVDA (but reduce position), TSM (picks-and-shovels survive)
3. SELL: Pure-play AI stocks with no profit (speculative names)
4. ROTATE INTO: non-AI tech with real earnings (AAPL), value stocks, healthcare
5. Add industrials (XLI) and defense (LMT) as rotation targets
6. DEPLOY CASH INTO AI when: P/E ratios return to historical averages AND capex plans are confirmed

**Key insight**: Even if AI is real (it is), valuations can still crash. The internet was real in 2000 but Amazon dropped 95%.

## Scenario 3: US Recession

**Trigger**: 2 consecutive quarters negative GDP + unemployment rising + yield curve uninversion

**Mechanism**: Consumer spending drops → corporate earnings decline → layoffs → more spending drops → stocks fall 20-35%

**Playbook**:
1. Increase cash to 25-30%
2. ROTATE FROM: consumer discretionary, small caps, high-beta tech
3. ROTATE INTO: consumer staples (XLP), healthcare (XLV), utilities (XLU)
4. KEEP: gold (counter-cyclical), defense (government spending independent)
5. ADD: dividend aristocrats for income during downturn
6. DEPLOY when: Fed starts emergency rate cuts + credit spreads peak

## Scenario 4: Energy Crisis / Oil Shock

**Trigger**: Oil > $120 sustained + Middle East supply disruption + OPEC cuts

**Mechanism**: Energy costs spike → inflation surges → consumer pain → Fed can't cut → stagflation risk

**Playbook**:
1. ADD: energy positions (XLE, oil majors)
2. ADD: nuclear (URA) — crisis accelerates nuclear investment case
3. REDUCE: consumer discretionary, airlines, logistics companies
4. KEEP: gold (inflation hedge), defense (geopolitical tensions)
5. Industrial stocks (CAT) benefit if crisis drives domestic energy infrastructure buildout

## Scenario 5: Stagflation

**Trigger**: Inflation > 5% + GDP < 1% + unemployment rising

**Mechanism**: Worst case — prices rising while economy shrinking. Traditional 60/40 portfolio fails because both stocks and bonds decline.

**Playbook**:
1. OVERWEIGHT: gold (15-20% of portfolio) — historically best stagflation asset
2. OVERWEIGHT: commodity producers, energy
3. OVERWEIGHT: companies with pricing power (monopolies, essential services)
4. REDUCE: growth stocks (P/E compression), long-duration bonds
5. REDUCE: anything dependent on consumer discretionary spending
6. KEEP: defense (government spending is inflation-resistant)
7. Cash in money market earns decent yield during high-rate environments

## Scenario 6: Bull Market Melt-Up

**Trigger**: VIX < 12 sustained + new highs daily + retail FOMO + IPO frenzy

**Mechanism**: Everyone is buying → prices rise → FOMO pulls in more buyers → parabolic rise → eventual crash

**Playbook**:
1. DO NOT GO ALL IN — this is when cash reserve matters most
2. Trim winning positions that have exceeded your target allocation
3. Build cash reserve to 25-30% (sell into strength)
4. Set trailing stop-loss alerts on largest positions
5. AVOID: chasing meme stocks, IPO day purchases, leveraged products
6. The time to be fearful is when others are greedy

## General Principles Across All Scenarios

- Act on the FIRST signal, not the third. By the time everyone sees it, the opportunity is gone.
- Sell in stages, not all at once. Markets often give multiple chances.
- Keep a written plan BEFORE the crisis. Emotional decisions in real-time are reliably bad.
- Cash is an OPTION on future opportunities. It's not "doing nothing."
- The best investors make their biggest moves during maximum pessimism.
