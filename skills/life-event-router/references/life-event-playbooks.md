# Life Event Financial Playbooks

## JOB CHANGE

### Immediate (Week 1)
1. **Document last-day details**: final paycheck date, PTO payout, bonus proration
2. **401k: stop contributions** at old employer (avoid over-contribution across plans)
3. **COBRA election**: you have 60 days to decide. Compare COBRA vs. marketplace vs. new employer
4. **HSA**: if losing HDHP coverage, stop HSA contributions (pro-rate the annual limit)
5. **Equity comp**: check vesting acceleration clauses, exercise windows for options (ISOs typically 90 days post-departure)

### Within 30 Days
6. **401k rollover decision**: stay in old plan (if low fees), roll to new plan, or roll to IRA
   - If considering Backdoor Roth: roll to new 401k (NOT IRA) to keep IRA empty for backdoor
   - If old plan has institutional funds with ultra-low fees, may be worth keeping
7. **Update beneficiaries**: all accounts (401k, IRA, life insurance, bank)
8. **New employer benefits enrollment**: maximize 401k match from day 1, enroll in ESPP, HSA, FSA
9. **Tax withholding**: adjust W-4 at new job (may need to over-withhold if started mid-year)

### Within 90 Days
10. **Exercise window**: ISOs expire 90 days after departure (typically). Decide: exercise or forfeit.
11. **Negotiate deferred comp**: if applicable, plan distribution timing
12. **Update financial plan**: new salary → new surplus → new routing

### Common Mistakes
- Forgetting to roll over 401k (orphaned accounts lose track)
- Missing ISO exercise window (90 days goes fast)
- Not adjusting tax withholding (underpayment penalty risk)
- Not maximizing new employer match from day 1

## RAISE / PROMOTION

### Immediate
1. **Calculate net increase**: gross raise → net after taxes at marginal rate
2. **Increase 401k contribution**: absorb 50-100% of net raise into 401k
3. **Check bracket impact**: did raise push you into new bracket? Affects Roth vs. Traditional decision
4. **Lifestyle inflation guard**: commit to investing the raise before spending it

### Within 30 Days
5. **Adjust W-4**: ensure withholding matches new income
6. **Re-evaluate Roth eligibility**: if income crossed $150K single / $236K MFJ, may need Backdoor Roth
7. **Update Freedom Number timeline**: higher income → faster freedom (if savings rate maintained)
8. **Max out remaining tax-advantaged space**: HSA, Mega Backdoor Roth

### Golden Rule
"Every raise is a wealth-building opportunity. The best time to increase savings is when income increases — you haven't adapted to the higher spending yet."

## HAVING A BABY

### Before Birth
1. **Health insurance**: add baby to plan within 30-day special enrollment period after birth
2. **Life insurance**: both parents need term life (10-20× income; 20-year term minimum)
3. **Will/trust**: name guardians for child (critical, don't delay)
4. **Dependent Care FSA**: enroll during open enrollment ($5,000/year pre-tax for childcare)

### Within 30 Days of Birth
5. **SSN for baby**: apply at hospital (needed for tax benefits)
6. **Update tax withholding**: additional dependent = lower tax
7. **Child Tax Credit**: $2,000/child (refundable up to $1,700)
8. **Start 529**: even small amounts — $50/month from birth compounds enormously

### Within 90 Days
9. **Beneficiary updates**: all accounts
10. **Estate plan**: update or create will/trust with guardian designation
11. **Disability insurance**: review coverage — you now have a dependent

### Financial Impact Modeling
- Average cost of raising a child to 18: ~$310K (USDA estimate, pre-college)
- College cost projection (18 years out): ~$200K-400K depending on school type
- 529 needed: ~$500-1,000/month from birth for full private university coverage
- Life insurance cost: ~$30-60/month for $1M 20-year term (healthy 30-year-old)

## MARRIAGE

### Before/During
1. **Filing status analysis**: compare MFJ vs MFS tax liability
2. **Prenup financial disclosures**: full asset and debt inventory (if applicable)
3. **Insurance consolidation**: combine auto, review health plan options

### Within 30 Days
4. **Beneficiary updates**: ALL accounts (this is the #1 most missed item)
5. **Name change processing**: SSA, bank accounts, brokerage, employer
6. **Tax withholding adjustment**: both spouses adjust W-4 for MFJ

### Within 90 Days
7. **Account strategy**: consolidate or keep separate based on preference
8. **Debt assessment**: combined debt picture, prioritize highest-rate payoff
9. **Emergency fund recalculation**: now based on combined essential expenses
10. **Estate plan**: wills, POA, healthcare directives
11. **Insurance review**: combined coverage needs, potential savings from bundling

## HOME PURCHASE

### Before Purchase
1. **DTI ratio**: total monthly debt payments / gross monthly income (target <36%)
2. **Down payment source**: taxable brokerage (tax implications of selling), gifts (gift letter needed), IRA ($10K first-time buyer exception)
3. **Credit optimization**: pay down revolving debt, don't open new accounts, don't close old accounts
4. **Rate lock timing**: lock when rate hits target (don't try to time the bottom)

### At Purchase
5. **Mortgage type**: 30yr fixed (stability) vs 15yr (wealth building) vs ARM (if short hold)
6. **Points analysis**: breakeven calculation (monthly savings × months to recoup cost)
7. **Property tax and insurance**: budget for escrow increases
8. **Title insurance**: always get owner's policy (one-time cost)

### After Purchase
9. **Homestead exemption**: file immediately (property tax reduction)
10. **PMI removal plan**: if <20% down, track when equity reaches 20%
11. **SALT deduction**: $10,000 cap on state/local + property tax deduction
12. **Mortgage interest deduction**: only valuable if total itemized > standard deduction

### Financial Impact
- Mortgage payment should be <28% of gross income (front-end ratio)
- Total debt service <36% of gross income (back-end ratio)
- True monthly cost = mortgage + property tax + insurance + maintenance (budget 1% of home value/year)

## JOB LOSS

### Immediate (empathy first, then action)
1. **File for unemployment**: don't delay, there's a waiting period in most states
2. **COBRA enrollment**: 60 days to decide (retroactive coverage)
3. **Emergency fund activation**: switch to essential-only spending
4. **Severance review**: negotiate if possible (consider asking for outplacement, COBRA subsidy, equity acceleration)

### Within 30 Days
5. **Health insurance**: compare COBRA ($$$) vs ACA marketplace (subsidies if income drops)
6. **401k**: do NOT cash out (10% penalty + income tax). Leave in place or roll to IRA.
7. **Budget recalculation**: map essential expenses, calculate runway
8. **Freelance/consulting**: file Schedule C if starting, set up estimated tax payments

### Within 90 Days
9. **Roth conversion opportunity**: low-income year = cheap Roth conversion (pay less tax on conversion)
10. **Tax-loss harvesting**: if markets are down, harvest losses to offset any severance income
11. **ACA subsidy optimization**: manage AGI to maximize premium tax credits

### Critical: Turn Crisis into Opportunity
Low-income years are GOLDEN for:
- Roth conversions (fill low brackets cheaply)
- Capital gains harvesting at 0% rate (if income <$47,025 single)
- Realizing gains to reset cost basis while in low bracket

## INHERITANCE

### Immediate
1. **Don't make major financial decisions for 6+ months** — grief affects judgment
2. **Step-up in basis**: inherited assets get cost basis reset to date-of-death value (huge tax benefit)
3. **Inherited IRA rules**: non-spouse must empty within 10 years (SECURE Act)
4. **Probate timeline**: can take 6-18 months depending on state

### Within 30 Days
5. **Inventory all assets**: bank accounts, brokerage, real estate, insurance, retirement accounts
6. **Estate attorney**: if estate is complex or contested
7. **Tax implications**: inheritance itself isn't taxable, but income FROM inherited assets is
8. **Beneficiary designations override wills** — verify all accounts

### Within 90 Days
9. **Investment plan for inherited assets**: don't sell hastily (step-up in basis means low/no tax on sale)
10. **Inherited IRA distribution strategy**: spread over 10 years to minimize bracket impact
11. **Real estate**: assess hold vs sell (stepped-up basis makes selling tax-efficient)
12. **Charitable giving**: if inclined, inherited appreciated assets are ideal for donation (double tax benefit)
