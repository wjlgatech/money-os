# Tax-Loss Harvesting Math

## How Tax Savings Are Calculated

### Net Capital Loss Application Order
1. Short-term losses offset short-term gains first
2. Long-term losses offset long-term gains first
3. Remaining net losses cross over (short offsets long, vice versa)
4. Up to $3,000 of NET capital losses offset ordinary income ($1,500 if married filing separately)
5. Remaining losses carry forward indefinitely

### Tax Savings by Type

**Short-term loss offsetting short-term gain:**
- Savings = Loss x ordinary income tax rate (10-37%)
- Example: $10,000 short-term loss at 32% bracket = $3,200 saved

**Long-term loss offsetting long-term gain:**
- Savings = Loss x long-term capital gains rate (0%, 15%, or 20%)
- Example: $10,000 long-term loss at 15% rate = $1,500 saved

**Net loss offsetting ordinary income ($3,000 cap):**
- Savings = min($3,000, net loss) x ordinary income tax rate
- Example: $3,000 at 32% bracket = $960 saved
- This is the MOST valuable use of losses

**NIIT savings (if applicable):**
- Additional 3.8% saved on investment income for high earners (MAGI > $200k/$250k)

### Worked Example

Portfolio has:
- $15,000 in realized long-term gains (from trimming winners)
- $8,000 in unrealized short-term losses (dead weight stocks)
- $12,000 in unrealized long-term losses (more dead weight)
- Investor in 32% ordinary / 15% LTCG bracket

Harvest all $20,000 in losses:
1. $8,000 ST loss offsets against $15,000 LT gain → saves $8,000 x 15% = $1,200
2. $12,000 LT loss offsets remaining $7,000 LT gain → saves $7,000 x 15% = $1,050
3. Remaining $5,000 LT loss: $3,000 offsets ordinary income → saves $3,000 x 32% = $960
4. $2,000 carries forward to next year

**Total tax saved this year: $3,210**
**Carryforward: $2,000**

### The Deferral Caveat

Tax-loss harvesting is primarily a TAX DEFERRAL, not permanent tax elimination:
- When you sell a loser and buy a replacement, the replacement has a lower cost basis
- When you eventually sell the replacement, you'll have a larger gain
- The benefit is the TIME VALUE of the deferred tax payment
- Exception: if you hold the replacement until death, the cost basis steps up (permanent savings)

### When Harvesting Is Most Valuable

1. You have large realized gains in the same tax year to offset
2. You're in a high tax bracket now but expect lower bracket later (retirement)
3. The position is dead weight you'd sell anyway (harvesting + cleanup)
4. You're near year-end and can use the $3,000 ordinary income offset
5. You can hold the replacement long enough to avoid short-term gains on it

### When Harvesting Is NOT Worth It

1. Transaction costs exceed tax benefit (very small positions)
2. You'd trigger wash sale because you want to rebuy immediately
3. Position is in tax-advantaged account (no tax benefit)
4. You're in the 0% LTCG bracket (no tax to save)
5. The position has strong recovery potential and you'd lose upside by selling
