# Cash Reserve Rules

## Strategic Cash Reserve (for active investors)

The cash reserve serves as "dry powder" — ammunition to buy quality assets during market panics.

### Sizing Rules

| Market Condition | Recommended Cash % | Rationale |
|-----------------|-------------------|-----------|
| Bull market, low VIX (<15) | 20-25% | Valuations stretched, correction likely |
| Normal market, moderate VIX (15-25) | 15-20% | Standard reserve |
| Bear market / crash, high VIX (>30) | 5-10% | Deploy cash into fear — this is what you saved for |
| Post-crash recovery | 10-15% | Rebuild reserve as positions recover |

### Where to Hold Cash

Ranked by yield + liquidity:
1. **Brokerage money market** (e.g., Fidelity SPAXX, Schwab SWVXX): ~4-5% yield, instant access
2. **Treasury bills** (via ETF like SHV, BIL): ~4-5% yield, same-day liquidity
3. **High-yield savings account**: ~4-5% yield, 1-day transfer
4. **Checking account**: 0% yield — avoid holding excess here

### Cash as Percentage of Total Portfolio

When calculating cash %, include ALL accounts:
- Brokerage cash + money market positions
- Settlement funds
- Do NOT count: emergency fund (separate purpose), 401k cash (limited access)

### Deployment Rules

When deploying cash during a dip:
- Deploy in tranches (25% of reserve per tranche), not all at once
- Set trigger levels: -10% from recent high = deploy tranche 1, -20% = tranche 2, etc.
- Prioritize highest-conviction positions first
- After full deployment, begin rebuilding reserve from new contributions

### Anti-Patterns

- **0% cash** ("fully invested"): No ability to capitalize on crashes. Historical crashes (2008, 2020, 2022) rewarded those with dry powder.
- **>40% cash**: Opportunity cost too high. Cash drag in a rising market compounds against you.
- **Cash in taxable, stocks in Roth**: Inverted. High-growth assets belong in Roth (tax-free gains). Cash earns taxable interest regardless of account type.

## Emergency Fund (separate from investment cash)

- 3-6 months of expenses in high-yield savings
- This is NOT part of the investment portfolio
- Do not count toward portfolio cash %
- Do not deploy during market crashes
