# Generational Wealth Account Types

## Custodial Roth IRA (BEST for working kids)

### Rules
- Child must have earned income (W-2 or self-employment)
- Contribution limit: lesser of $7,000 or total earned income (2025)
- Parent can FUND the contribution (doesn't have to be child's money)
- Must be opened as custodial until child reaches majority (18 or 21 by state)
- Conversions to regular Roth IRA at majority age

### Eligible Earned Income for Minors
- Part-time jobs (W-2 income)
- Babysitting, lawn mowing, tutoring (self-employment)
- Family business employment (must be reasonable pay for real work)
- Modeling, acting, social media income
- NOT: allowance, gifts, investment income

### Documentation
- Keep records of work performed and payment received
- For family business: formal employment records, reasonable market-rate pay
- File tax return for child if income requires it

## 529 Plan

### Rules
- No income limits for contributions
- No annual contribution limit (but subject to gift tax over $18K/year per person)
- Superfunding: contribute up to 5 years at once ($90K per person, $180K per couple)
- State tax deduction in 30+ states
- Tax-free growth and withdrawals for qualified education expenses
- New: roll unused to Roth IRA (up to $35K lifetime, 15-year minimum account age)

### Qualified Expenses
- Tuition (K-12 up to $10K/year; college unlimited)
- Room and board (if at least half-time student)
- Books, supplies, equipment
- Computer and internet access
- Student loan repayment (up to $10K lifetime)

### Strategy Matrix
| Scenario | Strategy |
|----------|----------|
| Child is 0-5 | Aggressive growth (100% equity) |
| Child is 6-12 | Moderate growth (70/30) |
| Child is 13-17 | Conservative (50/50 to 30/70) |
| Child is 18+ | Capital preservation if withdrawing soon |
| Won't use for college | Hold 15 years, roll to Roth IRA |

## UGMA/UTMA Custodial Accounts

### Rules
- No earned income requirement
- No contribution limits (but gift tax applies over $18K/year)
- UGMA: child takes control at 18 (most states)
- UTMA: child takes control at 18 or 21 (state-dependent)
- Irrevocable gift: once contributed, it's the child's money
- "Kiddie Tax": unearned income >$2,600 taxed at parent's rate (under age 19, or under 24 if student)

### When to Use
- Child has no earned income (can't do Roth IRA)
- Want to teach investing with real money
- Small amounts for hands-on learning
- Flexibility beyond education expenses

### When NOT to Use
- Large balances (FAFSA counts as student asset = hurts financial aid)
- Child may not be responsible with money at 18
- Better alternatives available (Roth IRA, 529)

## Comparison Matrix

| Feature | Custodial Roth | 529 | UGMA/UTMA |
|---------|---------------|-----|-----------|
| Earned income required | Yes | No | No |
| Contribution limit | $7,000/yr | None (gift tax) | None (gift tax) |
| Tax on growth | None (Roth) | None (education) | Kiddie tax rules |
| Withdrawal restrictions | Contributions any time | Education expenses | None at majority |
| Child gets control | At majority | Account owner decides | At majority (forced) |
| Financial aid impact | Minimal | Moderate (parent asset) | High (student asset) |
| Best for | Long-term wealth | Education funding | Teaching/flexibility |
| Recommended priority | 1st | 2nd | 3rd |
