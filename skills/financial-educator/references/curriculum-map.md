# Financial Literacy Curriculum Map

## Level 1: Foundation (Everyone Needs This)

### 1.1 Compound Interest — "The Eighth Wonder"
- Core concept: money earning money on money
- Rule of 72: years to double = 72 / return rate
- Show: $1,000 at 10% → $2,594 in 10yr → $6,727 in 20yr → $17,449 in 30yr
- Key insight: TIME is the most valuable variable, not amount
- Teaching moment triggers: first investment, any contribution discussion

### 1.2 Tax Brackets — "The Most Misunderstood Concept"
- MARGINAL vs EFFECTIVE rate (most people think "I'm in the 24% bracket" means all income taxed at 24%)
- 2025 brackets: 10%, 12%, 22%, 24%, 32%, 35%, 37%
- Example: $90K income → effective rate ~17%, marginal rate 22%
- Key insight: earning more NEVER makes you take home less (bracket only applies to income ABOVE the threshold)
- Teaching moment triggers: raise, bonus, Roth conversion discussion

### 1.3 Time Value of Money
- $1 today > $1 tomorrow (because you can invest it)
- Present value vs future value
- Why debt is expensive: you're paying for past consumption with future dollars
- Why investing early matters more than investing a lot
- Teaching moment triggers: any savings/investment discussion

### 1.4 Risk vs Reward
- Higher expected returns require accepting higher volatility
- Stocks: ~10% avg return, but -40% in worst years
- Bonds: ~5% avg return, but rarely lose >10%
- Cash: ~4% (currently), never loses nominal value
- Key insight: risk is only dangerous if you SELL during downturns
- Teaching moment triggers: market volatility, portfolio review

### 1.5 Inflation — "The Silent Tax"
- 3% inflation = your money loses half its value every 24 years
- $100K in savings at 0% → $55K purchasing power in 20 years
- This is WHY you must invest (just to stay even)
- Real return = nominal return - inflation
- Teaching moment triggers: cash holding discussion, HYSA rates

### 1.6 Emergency Fund
- Purpose: buy time and prevent forced selling during crisis
- Size: 3-6 months essential expenses (not gross income)
- Where: HYSA or short-term treasuries (liquid, safe, earning interest)
- When to use: involuntary job loss, medical emergency, essential home repair
- NOT for: vacations, car upgrades, "opportunities"
- Teaching moment triggers: job loss discussion, large unexpected expense

## Level 2: Intermediate (Active Investors)

### 2.1 Asset Allocation
- The mix of stocks, bonds, cash, and alternatives
- Drives 90% of portfolio return variation (more important than stock picking)
- Age-based rule of thumb: 120 - age = stock percentage
- Rebalance when allocation drifts >5% from target
- Teaching moment triggers: portfolio review, rebalancing discussion

### 2.2 Tax-Advantaged Accounts
- **401k/403b**: Pre-tax contributions reduce current tax; employer match = free money
- **Traditional IRA**: Tax deduction now, taxed on withdrawal
- **Roth IRA/401k**: No deduction now, tax-free growth and withdrawal forever
- **HSA**: Triple tax advantage (deduction + tax-free growth + tax-free medical withdrawal)
- Key insight: the ACCOUNT TYPE matters as much as what's inside it
- Teaching moment triggers: any contribution or account discussion

### 2.3 Dollar-Cost Averaging
- Invest same $ amount at regular intervals regardless of price
- Buys more shares when cheap, fewer when expensive
- Removes emotion from investing
- Works best with volatile assets over long periods
- Teaching moment triggers: new investment plan, windfall deployment

### 2.4 Index Funds vs Active Management
- Index funds own the whole market (or a segment) at ultra-low cost
- 85-90% of active managers underperform their index over 15+ years
- Expense ratio matters: 0.03% (VOO) vs 1.0% (average active) = huge gap over time
- On $100K over 30yr at 10% gross: 0.03% fee → $1.72M; 1.0% fee → $1.32M; fee drag = $400K
- Teaching moment triggers: fund selection, fee discussion

### 2.5 Rebalancing
- WHY: asset classes drift over time; winners become overweight
- WHEN: when any asset class is >5% off target, or at least annually
- HOW: sell overweight, buy underweight — preferably in tax-advantaged accounts
- TAX-SMART: rebalance by directing new contributions to underweight classes
- Teaching moment triggers: portfolio drift detected, new contributions

### 2.6 Dividend Reinvestment
- DRIP: automatically reinvest dividends to buy more shares
- Creates compound-on-compound effect
- In taxable accounts: dividends are still taxed even if reinvested
- Best strategy: DRIP in Roth (tax-free compounding); take cash in taxable if in high bracket
- Teaching moment triggers: dividend discussion, income vs growth

## Level 3: Advanced (Wealth Builders)

### 3.1 Tax-Loss Harvesting
- Sell losing positions to offset gains, saving tax
- Replace with similar (not identical) position to maintain exposure
- Wash sale rule: can't buy back same security within 30 days
- Carry forward unused losses ($3K/year against ordinary income, unlimited against gains)
- Teaching moment triggers: tax harvest opportunity, year-end planning

### 3.2 Asset Location
- WHERE you hold matters as much as WHAT you hold
- Taxable: tax-efficient assets (index funds, muni bonds, long-term holds)
- Traditional IRA: tax-inefficient assets (bonds, REITs — would be taxed at ordinary rates anyway)
- Roth: highest-growth assets (compound tax-free forever)
- HSA: highest-growth assets (triple tax advantage)
- Teaching moment triggers: account structure analysis, new investment

### 3.3 Roth Conversion Strategy
- Move Traditional IRA → Roth IRA, pay tax now for tax-free growth later
- Optimal in low-income years (job gap, early retirement, sabbatical)
- "Fill the bracket": convert up to the top of your current bracket
- Watch for: IRMAA (Medicare premium increase), ACA subsidy impact
- Teaching moment triggers: low-income year, tax planning

### 3.4 Equity Compensation
- RSU: taxed as ordinary income at vest, then capital gains on appreciation
- ISO: no tax at exercise (but AMT!), favorable LTCG if held 1yr post-exercise + 2yr post-grant
- NSO: taxed as ordinary income at exercise on the spread
- ESPP: typically 15% discount, optimal strategy depends on holding period
- Teaching moment triggers: vest event, job change, exercise decision

### 3.5 Concentration Risk
- HHI index measures portfolio concentration (sum of squared weights)
- Single position >15% = elevated risk; >25% = dangerous
- Correlated positions (NVDA + AMD + TSM) = effectively one bet
- Trim strategies: tax-aware, gradual, with replacement positions
- Teaching moment triggers: portfolio review, single-stock discussion

### 3.6 Safe Withdrawal Rate
- 4% rule: withdraw 4% of portfolio in year 1, adjust for inflation each year
- 95% success rate over 30-year periods (Trinity Study)
- For early retirement (40+ years): use 3.0-3.5%
- Sequence-of-returns risk: bad markets early in retirement are devastating
- Mitigation: 2-3 year cash buffer, flexible spending, part-time income
- Teaching moment triggers: freedom number calculation, retirement planning
